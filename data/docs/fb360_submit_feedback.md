# How to submit client feedback in FB360

## Overview
FB360 (Feedback 360) is the system for capturing, managing, and acting on client feedback. Unlike UAT which tracks issues and problems, FB360 captures opinions, suggestions, and feature desires — the qualitative signal that drives product direction. Every piece of client feedback is valuable, and capturing it accurately in FB360 ensures it reaches the right people.

## Who submits feedback
- Support leads capturing feedback during client calls
- Account managers receiving feedback during business reviews
- Clients submitting feedback directly through the FB360 client portal
- Market researchers entering structured feedback from research studies

## Step-by-step instructions

### Step 1 — Log in to FB360
Navigate to the FB360 portal and sign in with your enterprise credentials. The FB360 dashboard shows recent feedback entries, trending topics, and your assigned feedback items.

### Step 2 — Click "New Feedback"
Click the **New Feedback** button in the top right corner. The feedback creation form opens.

### Step 3 — Select the client
Choose the client from the dropdown. If the client is not in the list, contact your administrator.

### Step 4 — Select the feedback type
Choose the type that best describes the feedback:
- **Feature Request** — the client wants a capability that does not currently exist
- **Bug Report** — the client is reporting a problem (consider also logging in UAT)
- **Satisfaction** — the client is expressing satisfaction or dissatisfaction with a feature
- **NPS** — Net Promoter Score feedback from a formal survey

### Step 5 — Select the sentiment
Choose the overall tone of the feedback:
- **Positive** — the client is happy, complimentary, or enthusiastic
- **Neutral** — the client is informational, making a request without strong emotion
- **Negative** — the client is frustrated, critical, or threatening to churn

### Step 6 — Link to a feature (optional)
If the feedback relates to an existing feature or a known feature request, link it using the **Link feature** button. This associates the feedback with the feature's revenue signal.

### Step 7 — Write the feedback description
In the Description field, capture the client's feedback as accurately as possible. Use the client's own words where possible. Include:
- What the client said (paraphrased or direct quote)
- The context in which they gave the feedback
- Any specific examples they mentioned
- Their emotional tone

### Step 8 — Enter revenue opportunity
If this feedback relates to a feature request, enter the estimated revenue opportunity in USD — how much revenue could be generated if this feedback is acted upon. This figure is used by Sales and Marketing when assessing feature prioritisation.

### Step 9 — Submit
Click **Submit**. The feedback is saved and assigned a unique Feedback ID.

## After submission
- The feedback appears in the FB360 dashboard immediately.
- If the revenue opportunity exceeds $200,000, the product team is notified automatically.
- Market researchers review new feedback weekly to identify emerging patterns.

## Tips
- Capture feedback as soon as possible after the client interaction — details fade quickly.
- Never dismiss negative feedback as complaining. Negative feedback is often the most actionable.
- If a client gives both positive and negative feedback in the same conversation, create two separate entries.
